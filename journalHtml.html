Chronology of Events:

  1. Rescuing the Enchanted
    @JournalEntry[Rol Vovrisk]{Rol} goes upstairs to the room with the merchant's daughter, who tries to offer him sexual services. @JournalEntry[Rol Vovrisk]{Rol} realizes she is under the influence of a spell and attempts to dispel it, without success. He discovers that the girl's necklace is the source of the spell and calls @JournalEntry[Ragnard (Azuth)]{Ragnard} for help. @JournalEntry[Ragnard (Azuth)]{Ragnard} identifies the necklace as belonging to the Goddess Leviator and indicates that a powerful spell is needed to break the enchantment.

  2. A Costly Proposal
    @JournalEntry[Rol Vovrisk]{Rol} decides to write a letter under the name of @JournalEntry[Bazzuc]{Bazzuc} to purchase the merchant's daughter. The offer is accepted for 30,000 gold coins, making @JournalEntry[Rol Vovrisk]{Rol} the owner of the daughter.

  3. Departure to Dynnegal
    Upon leaving the bar, the group spots a large galleon bearing Ann's crest in the port. They decide to head to @JournalEntry[Dynnegall]{Dynnegall}.

  4. A Smelly Welcome
    Upon their arrival in @JournalEntry[Dynnegall]{Dynnegall}, they notice a strong nauseating smell of excrement in the streets. They head to the blacksmith to inform him about the price reduction in Kork by the Iron Guild, thereby securing the contract to help @JournalEntry[Othran Strongwill]{Othran} during his trial.

  5. Whispers of Compromise
    @JournalEntry[Bazzuc]{Bazzuc} hears rumors that @JournalEntry[Chevalier Jerold du Mont Noir, Magistrat de Siamorphe-Dynnegall-Dynnegall]{Jerold du Mont Noir} might possess a witness capable of compromising them with the story about bribing the guards of the city of Moray, which caused mutations due to tRol wine. Another rumor suggests that the residents want to eliminate the waste in the streets.

  6. Assassination in Shadows
    Faced with rumors of the capture of a guard who could compromise the Iron Cruisers, @JournalEntry[Bazzuc]{Bazzuc} goes to the prison to assassinate this guard. He turns invisible, puts the guards and the witness to sleep, writes a suicide letter, and makes the witness drink a corrosive poison to prevent any possibility of speaking after his death.

  7. Strike Negotiations
    The group heads to the district where the waste collectors are on strike. @JournalEntry[Rol Vovrisk]{Rol} convinces them to resume work in exchange for a certain amount of money. During the exchange, the group discovers that the strikers are racist towards the orcs and may have even killed one of them.

Conclusion:

Our adventure led us through a series of morally complex challenges, beginning with an insidious enchantment that ensnared a merchant's daughter and ending with a tense negotiation amid a waste collector strike. The tangled web of spells, betrayal, and negotiations tested our resolve as we navigated the underbelly of Dynnegal.
